About Me

Hello, I am Erickson M. Manabat. I live in Barangay Bago, General Tinio, Nueva Ecija. I am the son of Mr. Enrico Manabat and Ms. Sherlyn Manabat, and I am the second of six children. My eldest brother is Rennier Manabat; he is no longer studying. After me is my sister, Angilyn Manabat, a Grade 11 ABM student. Next is my brother, Enrico Manabat Jr., who is in Grade 9, followed by my little sister, Angela Manabat, who is four years old. Our youngest, Jandy Manabat, has passed away and is now in a better place, free from pain.


I studied at Nazareth Elementary School because we used to live in Barangay Nazareth. I almost didn't graduate from Grade 6 because I spent too much time watching anime on GMA, but thanks to my parents guidance, I went back to school. Even though I felt embarrassed after being absent for about three weeks, I was still able to graduate.


For high school, I attended Lino P. Bernardo National High School. Until Grade 8, I walked to school because I didn't have a motorcycle. When COVID-19 began, we shifted to modular learning. By Grade 9, I wasn't able to choose a strand. Since my older brother took SMAW, the school assigned me SMAW modules as my major, and I continued SMAW until Grade 12


From Grades 10 to 12, I stayed in Bago, which was closer to school than Nazareth. We lived in the place where my father was assigned as a worker and caretaker. After graduating from Grade 12, I took the NEUST Papaya Off-Campus entrance exam and passed. I entered college as a first-year student, and now I am starting my second year. I hope to pass again so I can achieve my dreams. I may not be the smartest, but I am determined to finish my studies for the sake of my future

My achievement in grade 12


One of my first achievements was serving as the leader of Group 3 in a research project. Our group received the Outstanding Output Award for our study entitled The Use of Wall-Mounted Tables to Enhance Limited Space. This project was later donated to one of the classrooms of Grade 11 ACAD-A at Lino P. Bernardo National High School, where I completed my secondary education.


Once again, I served as the leader of our research titled The Use of a Multipurpose Podium in Assisting Class Discussions. This study also won the Outstanding Output Award in research. Similar to our first project, this research was donated to the SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding) strand, where I completed my senior high school education. The project was suggested by my SMAW-12 adviser, and I personally designed the project layout on two bond papers for the final research title presentation.


I received the Outstanding Presenter Award for our second research project. I was very happy each time we finished a defense, especially since all of my defenses were successfully passed. This award was something I did not expect to achieve. However, I did anticipate winning the Outstanding Output Award because our strand, TVL-A (SMAW), was the only one that produced multifunctional projects such as the Multipurpose Podium.


The last achievement i list is receiving Honor Certificates during my Grade 12 year. In the first quarter, I ranked second in the class with an average of 90. In the second quarter, my average increased to 91,followed by 92 in both the third and final quarters. From the first to the last quarter, I consistently maintained the Top 2 position in our class.Despite this, I still held the highest overall average in the TVL-A (SMAW) strand, with a final average of 92. This became a very meaningful accomplishment for me because I was able to graduate with a medal for the first time. I had promised myself that I would graduate with a medal, and I achieved it. My next goal is to graduate with a degree in Information Technology, and I am determined to reach it.
